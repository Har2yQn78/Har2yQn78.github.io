# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

This is a personal site, not a recruiter funnel. The audience is fellow
builders, friends, and curious readers who land on it from X, GitHub, or a
shared link — plus Harry himself, since the site is partly a home for his own
writing and identity. They arrive curious about *who this person is and what he
thinks*, not to evaluate a hire. Context: a few minutes of attention, desktop
or mobile, following a thread of curiosity. The job to be done is expression and
connection, not conversion: state a clear point of view and reward the ones who
stay to read (the blog). Success is a visitor who leaves with a specific
impression of Harry and reads a post — never a filled-out contact form.

## Product Purpose

A personal brand site for an Intelligent Systems Engineer — part identity
statement, part writing home. It exists to make one person legible: the way he
thinks (systems), the way he works (honest, curious, relentless), and the
things he builds (backend, AI, tools, games). Success is a visitor who leaves
with a clear, specific impression of Harry — not "another dev portfolio" — and
who reads a blog post or follows a link. The writing is the payload; the design
is the frame that makes people trust it enough to read.

## Brand Personality

Rigorous, honest, relentless — a builder whose mind runs on games, tech, Linux,
and philosophy. Voice is first-person, reflective, plain-spoken; thinks in
systems and metaphors, closes essays on a quiet note. No hype, no performance of
expertise: states real depth *and* real limits ("C# never went deep", "PyTorch
not so deep") because credibility beats impressiveness. Emotional target:
respect and curiosity, not dazzle. The feeling should be *this person is the
real thing, and still climbing.*

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS/startup**: rounded cards, gradient blobs, pastel palettes,
  hero-metric templates, tracked-uppercase eyebrows above every section.
- **Corporate LinkedIn-brand**: stock photos, buzzwords, safe corporate blue,
  the polished-but-soulless personal-brand template.
- **Overdesigned/flashy**: glassmorphism everywhere, rainbow gradients, motion
  for its own sake, effects that shout instead of design.
- **Cluttered dev-portfolio**: skill bars, tech-logo grids, project card walls,
  badges — the default engineer-portfolio look.

## Design Principles

- **Practice the thesis.** The site is built the way he builds: every element
  understood and earning its place, no line copied without a reason. The craft
  itself is the argument.
- **Honesty over hype.** Show real depth and real limits. Never inflate. The
  restraint is the credibility.
- **Show the climb.** Endless Ascent is the frame — work-in-progress honesty
  over polished finality. Perpetual motion, never "arrived".
- **Signal, not friction.** Terminal-minimal. Every binding, section, and word
  removes a friction or carries meaning; clutter is a different kind of mess.
- **Systems, not decoration.** Coherence and interaction over ornament. Effects
  (ASCII fields, motion) exist because they mean something, not to fill space.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1 against the near-black surface.
Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` on every animation (already wired in Hero/About
via `useReducedMotion`) — heavy ASCII/starfield motion must degrade to a static
or crossfade fallback. Keyboard-navigable links, honest focus states.
